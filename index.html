Where would you like to display or run the ads within your content or video?
Choose the ads, and you can select multiple ad placements. Learn more.

Pre-roll Ads: Ads that play before the main content starts. See more.

These ads are shown to viewers before they engage with the video, ensuring that the advertisement is seen before the content begins.

Mid-roll Ads: Ads that appear during the middle of the content. See more.

Often placed during natural breaks or transitions. They are effective for longer videos as they capture viewers' attention partway through, but they can interrupt the viewing experience.

Post-roll Ads: Ads that play after the main content ends. See more.

These ads target viewers who have watched the entire video, making them ideal for engaging those who are already fully engaged with the content.

Banner Ads: Visual ads displayed on the screen, typically at the top or bottom. See more.

They can be static or interactive, providing a less intrusive way to display advertisements while viewers consume content.

Skippable Ads: These ads can be skipped by viewers after a few seconds. See more.

They are designed to be less intrusive and can provide a better user experience, but they may generate less revenue as viewers might skip them before the ad finishes.

Non-Skippable Ads: These ads must be watched in full before the content plays. See more.

They can be more lucrative due to their guaranteed viewership, but they may affect viewer retention by disrupting the content experience.
